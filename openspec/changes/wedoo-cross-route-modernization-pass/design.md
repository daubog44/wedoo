## Design Approach

This pass is not another literal Figma translation sprint. It is a cross-route refinement pass guided by:

1. existing Figma hierarchy
2. `DESIGN.md`
3. real browser output

The goal is to make the whole prototype feel like one credible product.

## Shared Direction

- reduce gratuitous glass and blur
- tighten border radius and shadow choices to a smaller set of repeated values
- make buttons, cards, and fields feel related across public and portal routes
- shorten supporting copy so headings and CTAs do more work
- keep editorial energy through composition and color, not through random effects

## Public Surfaces

- home, info, articles, and podcast should read as editorial product surfaces, not landing-page leftovers
- supporting paragraphs should be shorter and easier to scan
- cross-link sections should feel purposeful and compact

## Portal Surfaces

- dashboard hero blocks should be shorter, clearer, and less inflated
- side rails and cards should feel like parts of the same canvas
- navbar and action controls should feel product-grade, not placeholder-grade

## Stability Rules

- avoid hover scaling that changes composition too aggressively
- reserve image space and keep CTA placement stable
- prefer quieter skeletons and stable panel heights
- keep full-page parity only where the whole route is the real frame target
